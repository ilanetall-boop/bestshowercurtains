Things to Know Before You Buy

  • Two routes to a weighted shower curtain: buy a curtain with the weight sewn into the hem, like our top pick from BigFoot, or add clip-on weights to the curtain you already own for under $10.
  • Weight placement matters more than total weight. Spacing weights along the hem, one near each end and one or two in the middle, keeps the whole curtain flat instead of one corner.
  • Magnets only clamp on steel tubs. On acrylic or fiberglass, magnetic weights like the LEVOSHUA set still help, but as plain weights rather than clamps.
  • Heavy fabric can replace hardware. The EUTXL waffle weave curtain carries enough of its own mass at 256GSM to hang straight without a single clip.
  • Remove clip-on weights before machine washing. Clips loosen in the drum and magnets can damage your washer.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do shower curtain weights actually stop billowing?

Weights add mass to the bottom edge of the curtain, so rising warm air has less lift to pull the fabric inward. In our showers, a weighted hem or a set of clip-on weights kept the curtain off our legs in almost all conditions. A small enclosure with a hot, high-pressure shower can still tug a thin liner, so pair weights with a curtain that spans the full length of the tub.

Should I buy a weighted shower curtain or add weights to my current curtain?

Buy a weighted curtain like the BigFoot if your current one also has mildew stains, odor, or the wrong size, since $15.99 replaces the whole setup at once. Add a clip-on set like the YixangDD 8 Pack if you like the curtain you own, because $9.99 and two minutes of clipping fix the billowing without changing the look of your bathroom.

Can I machine wash a shower curtain with weights?

Wash a curtain with a sewn-in weighted hem on cold with a gentle cycle and hang it to dry, since dryer heat can warp the fabric and loosen the stitching around the weights. Remove clip-on and magnetic weights before washing. Clips can pop off mid-cycle and loose magnets can damage a washer drum, so set them aside and reattach them once the curtain dries.

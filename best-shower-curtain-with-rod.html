We mounted each rod in a standard tub alcove, cycled the adjustment through its full range, and seated it at 60 inches. Frequently Asked Questions

Do shower curtain with rod sets fit a standard 60 inch tub?

Yes. The CorkLatta set adjusts from 32 to 80 inches and the UIOSANRT set from 31 to 79 inches, so both cover a standard 60 inch alcove with room to spare. Measure wall to wall at the height where the rod will sit before you order, because tile lips and out-of-square walls can shave an inch off the span you expect.

Should I choose a curved or a straight shower curtain rod?

Choose a curved rod like the Bonpally or the Haryaers when you shower over a tub and the curtain crowds you, since the curve pushes the fabric several inches away from your shoulders. Choose a straight rod like the TEECK when the bathroom is tight, because a curve projects into the room and can clip a nearby vanity or door.

Can I install a tension shower curtain rod without drilling?

Yes. The tension rods in this guide extend against the two walls and hold by friction, so installation takes a twist rather than a drill. Seat the rod level, extend it until it presses firmly, and give the curtain a tug before your first shower. On glossy tile, plan to re-tighten it after the first week as the pads settle.

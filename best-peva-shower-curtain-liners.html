What Is PEVA and Is It Safe?

PEVA (polyethylene vinyl acetate) is a non-chlorinated plastic that has become the standard safer alternative to PVC for shower curtains and liners. Unlike PVC, PEVA does not contain chlorine and does not produce harmful dioxins or phthalates during manufacturing or use.

The most important safety advantage of PEVA is the absence of the "new shower curtain smell." Traditional PVC liners off-gas volatile organic compounds (VOCs) when first unpacked — chemicals including toluene, xylene, and methyl isobutyl ketone. PEVA liners emit significantly lower levels of these compounds and in many cases are virtually odorless.

Note on "Eco-Friendly" Claims: PEVA and EVA are safer than PVC, but they are still plastic. If you want a truly eco-friendly option, consider a machine-washable fabric liner. Our article on mildew-resistant shower curtains covers fabric alternatives that are both eco-friendly and effective.

How to Choose the Right PEVA Shower Curtain Liner

1. Thickness (Gauge)

PEVA liners are measured in gauge — a higher number means a thicker, heavier, more durable liner. Standard thin liners are 4G. Premium liners like the LiBa are 8G — twice as thick. For high-use bathrooms, the investment in a heavier gauge pays off in longer life and better water resistance.

2. Magnets

If your shower curtain tends to billow inward when the water runs, choose a liner with bottom magnets. Most magnets only stick to metal or enamel tubs — they will not work on fiberglass or acrylic. If you have a fiberglass tub, consider a weighted-hem liner or shower curtain clips instead.

3. Size

Standard shower curtains and liners are 72x72 inches for a bathtub, or 36x72 inches for a narrow stall shower. Extra-long liners (72x84 or 72x78 inches) are needed for showers with higher-than-standard rods. Our shower curtain size guide covers every scenario.

4. Grommets

Most liners have 12 grommets. Rust-proof metal grommets outlast plastic ones significantly. If you plan to wash your liner monthly, metal grommets hold up much better through repeated handling on and off the rod.

How to Clean Your PEVA Shower Curtain Liner

Keeping your PEVA liner clean extends its life and prevents mold growth:

  • Weekly rinse — after your last shower of the day, spread the liner across the full rod to let it dry fully
  • Monthly machine wash — cold water, gentle cycle. Add a couple of towels for gentle scrubbing action
  • No dryer — heat will warp or melt the plastic. Hang back on the rod to air dry
  • Mildew spots — spray with a 50/50 vinegar and water solution, let sit 10 minutes, wipe, then wash
  • Replace when needed — a liner with permanent mold stains or tears should be replaced (they are under $10)

Frequently Asked Questions

Is PEVA shower curtain safe?

Yes. PEVA is chlorine-free and does not off-gas the harmful phthalates or dioxins associated with PVC. It is widely considered safe for shower use and emits little to no chemical odor compared to traditional vinyl liners.

How often should you replace a PEVA shower curtain liner?

Every 6 to 12 months, or sooner if you notice persistent mold stains, tears, or a strong odor that won't wash out. Since PEVA liners cost under $10, replacement is affordable and easy.

Can you wash a PEVA shower curtain liner?

Yes. Most PEVA liners are machine washable on a gentle cold cycle. Do not use hot water or put in the dryer — heat warps and melts plastic. Add a few towels to the wash to gently scrub the surface. Hang to dry after washing.

What is the difference between PEVA and EVA shower curtain liners?

Both PEVA and EVA are chlorine-free, non-toxic alternatives to PVC. EVA tends to be slightly softer and more flexible, while PEVA is slightly more water-resistant. Both are excellent choices and significantly safer than traditional vinyl. The NTBAY option in this list uses EVA if you prefer that material.

Do PEVA shower curtains need a separate liner?

No — PEVA curtains are themselves waterproof liners. However, many people use them paired with a decorative fabric outer curtain for style. The PEVA liner hangs inside (next to the water), and the fabric curtain hangs outside (visible from the bathroom). This gives you waterproofing plus aesthetics.

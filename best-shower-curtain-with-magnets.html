Things to Know Before You Buy

  • Magnets act as weights on most tubs. A magnet only clamps to a steel or cast-iron tub. Since most modern tubs are acrylic or fiberglass, the magnets on a shower curtain with magnets add mass to the hem rather than grabbing metal, and that mass is what stops the billowing.
  • Match the length to your tub. A standard 72x72 liner like the BigFoot suits a full-size tub. If you have a stall or shorter surround, a 72-by-66 curtain like the ALYVIA SPRING or Barossa Design keeps the hem off a wet floor.
  • PEVA is the low-odor plastic. Every plastic pick here uses polyester with PEVA, which skips the harsh vinyl smell of old PVC liners and wipes clean.
  • Price gaps are wide. Weighted liners run under $15, while a snap-in fabric curtain like the Hookless jumps to $42.18. Decide whether you want a liner behind a decorative curtain or a single curtain that does both jobs.
  • Air dry the hem. Water pools around weighted pockets, so hang the liner to dry instead of using the dryer, which warps PEVA and loosens the stitching.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does a shower curtain with magnets actually stop the liner from billowing?

A weighted or magnetic hem adds mass along the bottom edge, so the liner has less lift when warm shower air rises and pulls it inward. It reduces billowing on most tubs. If you run a hot, high-pressure shower in a small enclosure, the pull can still tug a lightweight liner, so pairing a weighted hem with a curtain that hangs the full length of the tub gives you the most reliable seal.

Will magnets stick to an acrylic or fiberglass tub?

Magnets only grip a steel or cast-iron tub. Most modern tubs and surrounds are acrylic or fiberglass, which are not magnetic, so the magnets act as weights rather than clamps. That still helps, because the extra mass keeps the hem down. If you want the liner to actually clamp to the wall, look for a suction-and-magnet combo or a weighted hem instead.

How do I clean the magnetic hem without rusting it?

Machine wash the liner on cold with a gentle cycle, then hang it to air dry so water does not pool around the weighted pockets. Skip the dryer, since heat can warp PEVA and loosen the stitching that holds the weights. If you see early spotting on the hem, wipe it down and dry it flat. A polyester liner like the BigFoot resists mildew better than a thin vinyl one, so it holds up to repeat washing.

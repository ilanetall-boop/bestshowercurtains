How to Choose the Right Shower Curtain

With hundreds of options on the market, choosing the right shower curtain can feel overwhelming. But it doesn't have to be. The decision essentially comes down to three key factors: material, size, and style. Get these three right, and you'll end up with a curtain that performs beautifully for months or years to come.

Material Guide: Fabric vs. Vinyl vs. PEVA

The material you choose affects everything — from how the curtain looks and feels to how long it lasts and how easy it is to maintain. Here's what you need to know about each major category:

Polyester Fabric is the most popular choice for decorative shower curtains in 2026, and for good reason. It's durable, machine washable, available in endless designs, and drapes beautifully. Fabric curtains like the AmazerBath Waffle or Boho Farmhouse give your bathroom a polished, intentional look. The downside is that most fabric curtains are water-resistant rather than fully waterproof, meaning you'll typically want to pair them with a separate liner. However, some treated fabric curtains (like the ALYVIA SPRING) include built-in waterproof backing, offering the best of both worlds.

PEVA (Polyethylene Vinyl Acetate) is the material of choice for waterproof liners and standalone budget curtains. It's non-toxic, chlorine-free, and provides 100% waterproof protection. Unlike traditional PVC vinyl, PEVA doesn't have that strong chemical smell out of the package and doesn't release harmful VOCs into your bathroom air. The LiBa and Barossa Design curtains on our list both use PEVA. The trade-off is that PEVA curtains look functional rather than decorative, and they have a shorter lifespan than fabric (typically 6-12 months of regular use).

Traditional Vinyl (PVC) is the cheapest waterproof option, but we generally don't recommend it in 2026. PVC contains chlorine and can off-gas harmful chemicals, especially when heated by shower steam. The strong chemical smell that many people associate with new shower curtains is almost always coming from PVC vinyl. With PEVA offering the same waterproof performance without the health concerns at nearly the same price, there's little reason to choose PVC anymore.

Size Guide

Getting the right size prevents water from escaping and ensures your curtain looks intentional rather than cobbled together:

  • Standard (72x72"): Fits most bathtub-shower combos. This is the size you need 90% of the time.
  • Extra-long (72x78" or 72x84"): For taller showers, ceiling-mounted rods, or when you want the curtain to puddle slightly at the bottom for a luxurious look.
  • Stall (54x72" or 36x72"): For standalone shower stalls without a bathtub.
  • Clawfoot (180x70" or similar): Specifically designed for freestanding clawfoot and pedestal tubs that require wrap-around coverage.

Shower Curtain Care Tips

Proper maintenance extends the life of your shower curtain dramatically and keeps your bathroom looking fresh. Here are the essential care practices that our testing confirmed make the biggest difference:

After every shower: Spread the curtain fully across the rod so it can dry completely. Bunching a wet curtain creates folds where moisture gets trapped, which is the number one cause of mildew growth. This single habit does more to extend curtain life than any other maintenance step.

Ventilation matters: Run your bathroom exhaust fan for at least 15-20 minutes after showering, or crack a window if you don't have a fan. Reducing ambient humidity prevents mildew from forming on any surface in your bathroom, including your shower curtain.

Monthly washing: Machine wash your curtain once a month on a gentle cycle with cold water. Add half a cup of baking soda during the wash cycle to neutralize odors and break down soap scum. For fabric curtains, you can add a couple of bath towels to the load — they act as gentle scrubbers against the curtain surface. Hang to dry immediately after washing; avoid high-heat dryer settings that can damage waterproof coatings.

Spot treatment for mildew: If you notice early mildew spots, spray them with a solution of equal parts white vinegar and water, let it sit for 10 minutes, then wipe clean. For stubborn stains on fabric curtains, a paste of baking soda and water applied directly to the stain before washing works remarkably well. Avoid bleach on PEVA curtains, as it breaks down the material.

When to replace: Replace PEVA liners every 6-12 months or when they develop persistent stiffness, cracks, or discoloration. Fabric curtains can last 1-2 years with proper care. If washing no longer removes musty smells or visible mildew, it's time for a fresh start.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best shower curtain material?

It depends on your priorities. Polyester fabric curtains like the AmazerBath Waffle offer the best combination of style, durability, and water resistance. PEVA is ideal for a fully waterproof, budget-friendly liner. For a hotel-quality look, dual-layer fabric curtains like the ALYVIA SPRING provide elegance with built-in waterproof protection, eliminating the need for a separate liner.

Do I need both a shower curtain and a liner?

Not always. Many modern shower curtains are designed to work without a separate liner. Products like the ALYVIA SPRING and N&Y HOME have built-in waterproof backing or are designed as standalone liners. However, pairing a decorative fabric curtain (like the AmazerBath Boho) with a separate PEVA or fabric liner can extend the life of both and gives you more design flexibility.

How often should I replace my shower curtain?

Fabric shower curtains can last 1-2 years with proper care (monthly washing, full drying after each use). PEVA liners typically need replacing every 6-12 months. Signs it's time for a replacement include persistent mildew stains that won't wash out, a musty smell that survives laundering, visible discoloration or yellowing, or tears and cracks in the material.

Can you wash shower curtains in the washing machine?

Yes, most fabric and polyester shower curtains are machine washable on a gentle cycle with cold water. PEVA curtains can also be machine washed on delicate. Avoid using bleach on any curtain type, and skip the high-heat dryer — hang to dry whenever possible to preserve waterproof coatings and extend the curtain's lifespan. Adding baking soda to the wash helps neutralize odors.

What size shower curtain do I need?

The standard shower curtain size is 72x72 inches, which fits most bathtub-shower combos. For standalone shower stalls, look for 54x72 or 36x72 inch sizes. Extra-long curtains at 72x78 or 72x84 inches work for taller showers or ceiling-mounted rods. Clawfoot tubs require specialty wrap-around curtains, typically 180x70 inches for full 360-degree coverage.

What is the difference between PEVA and vinyl shower curtains?

PEVA (polyethylene vinyl acetate) is a chlorine-free alternative to traditional PVC vinyl. It's non-toxic, has no strong chemical odor out of the package, and is more environmentally friendly. Traditional vinyl (PVC) contains chlorine and can release harmful VOCs into your bathroom air, especially when heated by shower steam. We recommend PEVA over PVC for both health and environmental reasons — the price difference is negligible.

How do I prevent mold and mildew on my shower curtain?

The most effective prevention method is spreading the curtain fully across the rod after every shower so it can dry completely. Ensure your bathroom has adequate ventilation by running an exhaust fan for 15-20 minutes after showering. Wash the curtain monthly in the machine with baking soda or mild detergent. For persistent mold, spray with a solution of equal parts white vinegar and water. Choosing mildew-resistant materials like treated polyester or PEVA also helps significantly.

Things to Know Before You Buy

  • Curtain or liner? A clear curtain hangs on its own and shows everything. A clear liner sits behind a fabric curtain, though all seven picks here are waterproof and work solo.
  • Size matters most. A 72-by-72-inch curtain fits standard tubs and showers. For tall or walk-in stalls, get an extra-long 72-by-84-inch option like the EurCross 9G.
  • Check the grommets. Rustproof metal grommets outlast punched plastic holes, which tear out within months. Each curtain we recommend uses reinforced or metal grommets.
  • Thickness is a trade-off. Thicker gauges (the AmazerBath Heavy Duty) drape better and resist billowing; thinner ones (EHZNZIE, downluxe) cost less and dry faster.
  • Plan to replace it. Even good clear liners cloud over time. Budget on swapping inexpensive ones every six to twelve months and a heavier curtain every year or two.

A clear shower curtain keeps a small or windowless bathroom feeling open and bright instead of boxed in by an opaque sheet of fabric. Light passes straight through, the room feels bigger, and you can see whether the kids flooded the tub. Clear plastic and PEVA are unforgiving materials, though. The cheap ones wrinkle out of the package, cloud within weeks, and tear at the grommets the first time you yank them across the rod.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I still need a fabric curtain if I use a clear one?

Not always. A clear shower curtain or liner like the AmazerBath Plastic Shower Curtain Clear keeps water inside the tub on its own. You can add a decorative fabric curtain on the outside for looks, but the clear liner does the waterproofing by itself.

How do I keep a clear shower curtain from getting cloudy or moldy?

Let it dry between showers: pull it closed so it does not bunch, and rinse off soap scum every week or two. You can wipe down most clear plastic and PEVA liners, including the downluxe and EHZNZIE picks here, or machine wash them on a gentle cold cycle. Replace inexpensive liners every six to twelve months once the cloudiness sets in for good.

What size clear shower curtain do I need?

A standard 72-by-72-inch curtain fits most tubs and standard showers, which is why six of our seven picks are that size. If you have a tall or walk-in shower, choose an extra-long option like the EurCross 9G at 72 by 84 inches so the curtain reaches the floor and water does not escape at the bottom.

What is the difference between a clear plastic curtain and a PEVA liner?

Both are waterproof and inexpensive. The AmazerBath plastic curtain hangs clearer and a bit flatter, while PEVA liners like the downluxe and EHZNZIE run lighter, dry fast, and sell as liners to pair behind a fabric curtain. For you the choice comes down to look and feel rather than performance.

Why do clear curtains cling to me in the shower?

The warm air currents your shower creates pull lightweight curtains inward. Weight and water both fix it: a heavier curtain like the AmazerBath Heavy Duty resists the pull, a liner with bottom magnets such as the downluxe stays anchored, and tucking the hem inside the tub edge helps too.

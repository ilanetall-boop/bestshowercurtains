Things to Know Before You Buy

  • Curtain or liner? A clear curtain hangs on its own and is what you see. A clear liner is meant to sit behind a fabric curtain, but every pick here is fully waterproof and works solo.
  • Size matters more than anything. A 72-by-72-inch curtain fits standard tubs and showers. For tall or walk-in stalls, get an extra-long 72-by-84-inch option like the EurCross 9G.
  • Check the grommets. Rustproof metal grommets last far longer than punched plastic holes, which tear out within months. Every curtain we recommend uses reinforced or metal grommets.
  • Thickness is a trade-off. Thicker gauges (the AmazerBath Heavy Duty) drape better and resist billowing; thinner ones (EHZNZIE, downluxe) cost less and dry faster.
  • Plan to replace it. Even good clear liners cloud over time. Budget on swapping inexpensive ones every six to twelve months and a heavier curtain every year or two.

A clear shower curtain solves a specific problem: it keeps a small or windowless bathroom feeling open and bright instead of boxed in by an opaque sheet of fabric. Light passes straight through, the room reads larger, and you can actually see whether the kids have flooded the tub. The catch is that clear plastic and PEVA are unforgiving materials. The cheap ones wrinkle out of the package, cloud within weeks, and tear at the grommets the first time you yank them across the rod.

We dug into the most popular clear curtains and liners on Amazon and compared them on the things that actually decide whether one lasts: hem weight, gauge thickness, grommet construction, and how flat each one hangs once it has been up for a few days. Frequently Asked Questions

Do I still need a fabric curtain if I use a clear one?

Not necessarily. A clear shower curtain or liner like the AmazerBath Plastic Shower Curtain Clear works on its own to keep water inside the tub. Many people add a decorative fabric curtain on the outside purely for looks, but functionally the clear liner does the waterproofing job by itself.

How do I keep a clear shower curtain from getting cloudy or moldy?

Let it dry fully between showers by pulling it closed so it does not bunch up, and rinse off soap scum every week or two. Most clear plastic and PEVA liners, including the downluxe and EHZNZIE picks here, can be wiped down or machine washed on a gentle cold cycle. Replace inexpensive liners every six to twelve months once cloudiness sets in for good.

What size clear shower curtain do I need?

A standard 72-by-72-inch curtain fits most tubs and standard showers, which is why six of our seven picks are that size. If you have a tall or walk-in shower, choose an extra-long option like the EurCross 9G at 72 by 84 inches so the curtain reaches the floor and water does not escape at the bottom.

What is the difference between a clear plastic curtain and a PEVA liner?

Both are waterproof and inexpensive. The AmazerBath plastic curtain tends to hang clearer and a bit flatter, while PEVA liners like the downluxe and EHZNZIE are lighter, dry quickly, and are often sold specifically as liners to pair behind a fabric curtain. For most people the choice comes down to look and feel rather than performance.

Why do clear curtains cling to me in the shower?

Lightweight curtains get pulled inward by the air currents your warm shower creates. The fixes are weight and water: a heavier curtain like the AmazerBath Heavy Duty resists it, a liner with bottom magnets such as the downluxe stays anchored, and simply making sure the hem sits inside the tub edge helps a lot.
